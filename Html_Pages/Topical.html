The Interplay between Technology and Society

  1. Key Term
    • Disruptive technology

      • Disruptive technology refers to any enhanced or completely new technology that disrupts or replaces an existing technology, rendering it obsolete.

    • Technology Determinism

      • The view that technology determines our values, culture, and way of life.

Because of our study within computer technology, we are dependent on the internet and not only us within this study, but according to data report there are a total of 5.30 billion people on the globe who use the internet and with 2% this number is getting bigger and bigger every year. Thus, by using satellite internet, the internet will be available to countries that today do not have access, which is very important if you want to have easy communication across other countries.

  3. Describe your immediate reactions
    • Expensive cheap. Satellite internet infrastructure can be expensive to develop and maintain, which can then affect pricing for consumers.
    • Larger communication networks that can connect people and organizations around the world.
    • Slow network - Satellite signals take longer because they have to travel to space before returning to Earth. This extra travel time makes the internet via satellite feel slower. It can be particularly noticeable when you use services such as video conferencing or online games that require a quick response.
    • National Security: Satellite Internet technology can enhance national security by providing strong communications networks, especially in non-urban or disaster areas.
  4. Describe three potentially positive aspects of this development
    • Satellites have ushered in a new era of secure and efficient online communication. By bouncing signals off satellites, data can be encrypted and transmitted worldwide securely, minimizing the risk of interference or tampering.
    • Satellite technology has further facilitated the development of cloud computing, allowing data to be securely stored in the cloud and accessed from any internet-connected device. This has also revolutionized the way businesses and individuals manage and share data, fostering greater global connectivity and collaboration
    • Technologies like Starlink can provide more freedom to users in countries that want to challenge censorship and restrictions, helping them circumvent strict censorship practices in China, for example.
