The Interplay between Technology and Society

  1. Key Term

    • Disruptive technology

      • Disruptive technology refers to any enhanced or completely new technology that disrupts or replaces an existing technology, rendering it obsolete.

    • Technology Determinism

      • The view that technology determines our values, culture, and way of life.

  3. Describe your immediate reactions

    • Expensive cheap. Satellite internet infrastructure can be expensive to develop and maintain, which can then affect pricing for consumers.

    • Larger communication networks that can connect people and organizations around the world.

    • National Security: Satellite Internet technology can enhance national security by providing strong communications networks, especially in non-urban or disaster areas.

    • Slow network - Satellite signals take longer because they have to travel to space before returning to Earth. This extra travel time makes the internet via satellite feel slower. It can be particularly noticeable when you use services such as video conferencing or online games that require a quick response.

  4. Describe three potentially positive aspects of this development

    • Satellites have ushered in a new era of secure and efficient online communication. By bouncing signals off satellites, data can be encrypted and transmitted worldwide securely, minimizing the risk of interference or tampering.

    • Satellite technology has further facilitated the development of cloud computing, allowing data to be securely stored in the cloud and accessed from any internet-connected device. This has also revolutionized the way businesses and individuals manage and share data, fostering greater global connectivity and collaboration

    • Technologies like Starlink can provide more freedom to users in countries that want to challenge censorship and restrictions, helping them circumvent strict censorship practices in China, for example.

    • Describe three potentially negative aspects of this development

      • Energy consumption is a major sustainability issue; satellite internet connections and equipment require large amounts of energy to operate, and this can be costly and have environmental impacts.

      • The satellites themselves require a lot of energy to operate and the launch process also uses large amounts of fuel. This fuel releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere and contributes to global warming.
      • The researchers found that the installation of satellite internet infrastructure had a significant impact on the local ecosystem. The presence of towers and other equipment not only increased the presence of non-native species such as birds, but also caused changes in the behavior of existing species. ​(Frąckiewicz, 2023)​
    • Make up your mind!

      • Better internet access: Satellite internet can make the internet available in areas where there was previously poor or no access. This helps people who live outside cities, for example those who live on a farm, gain access to information, education and job opportunities. Emergencies: Satellite internet is important for dealing with disasters and security situations. Global cooperation: Satellite internet provides the opportunity for simple and global communication. This helps science, diplomacy and trade across borders.     • Identify potential consequences

      • The establishment of the internet via satellites could lead to a reduction in the impact on nature. If a new large internet cable is to be laid, they could lead to encroachment on nature, internet via satellites avoids this. To protect the cables, they are often buried on the seabed. This can disturb the nearby ecosystem in a negative way (Bernhard, 2023).

      According to Howell and Pultarova (Tereza Pultarova, 2023), there are astronomers who are concerned that the shiny Starlink satellites could interfere with observing space. They also claim that the satellites can pose a collision hazard for spacecraft.

      • What might be unintended consequences?

        • Space Debris: Launching more satellites could contribute to an increase in space debris, which could pose a risk to other satellites and space activities.

      • Who might loose from this technology?

        • Traditional Internet Service Providers: Satellite internet can pose a threat to traditional Internet Service Providers (ISPs), especially in remote and underserved areas. Cable and Fiber Companies: In regions where satellite internet competes effectively, cable and fiber optic providers may experience a reduction in market share.

      • Who might benefit from this technology?

        • Rural and Remote Communities: Satellite internet can provide high-speed connectivity to areas where traditional broadband infrastructure is not practical or available.

      • Are there environmental consequences?

        • Yes. Satellite internet infrastructure requires large amounts of energy for satellite operations and data centers, which can increase energy consumption and associated environmental impacts.

      • Are there social consequences?

        • The widespread use of satellite internet may raise concerns about user privacy and potential surveillance, depending on the policies and regulations in place.

      • Are there economic consewuences?

        • Satellite internet can bring competition to the Internet Service Providers market, lower prices and improve service quality
